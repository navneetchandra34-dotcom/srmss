Odisha, India

Dedicated to Vulnerable Communities

[span_129](start_span)

Since its inception in 2007 in Odisha, Subha Rashmi Mahila Seva Samiti has been working with rural and coastal communities to improve livelihoods, conserve natural resources, strengthen local institutions, and increase access to basic services for vulnerable households[span_129](end_span).

[span_130](start_span)

Over the years, the organization has expanded its work across several districts of Odisha while strictly maintaining its strong grassroots presence[span_130](end_span). [span_131](start_span)[span_132](start_span)Our work directly targets underserved populations facing geographic isolation, climate vulnerability, and economic challenges[span_131](end_span)[span_132](end_span).

Core Operational Landscapes

  • [span_133](start_span)[span_134](start_span)Coastal Fishing Villages: Supporting traditional fishing communities along the Odisha coastline with sustainable fisheries management[span_133](end_span)[span_134](end_span).
  • [span_135](start_span)[span_136](start_span)[span_137](start_span)Intertidal & Estuarine Habitats: Protecting critical spawning and breeding grounds for endangered marine species like *Tachypleus gigas* along beaches and riverbanks[span_135](end_span)[span_136](end_span)[span_137](end_span).
  • [span_138](start_span)[span_139](start_span)Salinity-Prone Communities: Addressing water contamination, salinity intrusion, and flooding through climate-resilient WASH infrastructure[span_138](end_span)[span_139](end_span).
  • [span_140](start_span)[span_141](start_span)Rural & Underserved Villages: Empowering women's Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and bringing primary healthcare screening closer to remote households[span_140](end_span)[span_141](end_span).
