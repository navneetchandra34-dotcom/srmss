Empowering Communities, Protecting Nature, Transforming Lives.

Established in 2007 in Odisha by Late Jyotna Rani Jena. We work alongside rural and vulnerable coastal communities to build resilient livelihoods, conserve endangered ecosystems, and champion women leadership.

Our Mission & Reach

We combine community participation with scientific evidence and local stewardship to advance sustainable livelihoods across coastal and rural Odisha.

Women Leadership

We empower women by strengthening Self-Help Groups (SHGs), developing vocational skills, and promoting participation in local governance.

Learn More →

Coastal Conservation

Protecting the endangered Indian horseshoe crab (Tachypleus gigas)—a "living fossil"—alongside community-led coastal waste reduction.

Learn More →

Sustainable Fisheries

Promoting responsible fishing practices, strengthening traditional fisher institutions, and providing alternative livelihood options.
