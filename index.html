Who We Are

Rooted in Grassroots Action & Participatory Development

Subha Rashmi Mahila Seva Samiti (SRMSS) began in 2007 as a community-based initiative designed to respond directly to the unique socioeconomic and environmental challenges faced by women, children, traditional fishing households, and vulnerable rural groups.

Over the years, our operational footprint has expanded across multiple districts of Odisha. Yet, our foundation remains unchanged: true sustainable development requires local communities to actively identify their priorities and take the lead in implementing solutions.

Read Our Full History

Our Guiding Vision

"A just, inclusive, and resilient society where every person, especially women, children, and marginalized communities, has the opportunity to live with dignity, realize their potential, and contribute to sustainable and equitable development."

Development is most effective when local communities are directly involved in identifying their priorities and taking part in the solutions.

What We Do

Our Six Holistic Intervention Areas

We implement comprehensive, community-led programmes across six foundational thematic pillars to foster economic resilience, protect marine biodiversity, and improve public health.

Sustainable Fisheries

Securing the livelihoods of coastal fishing communities while protecting marine ecosystems through responsible resource management.

  • Community-based fisheries management.
  • Promotion of responsible fishing practices.
  • Alternative livelihood support during seasonal fishing ban periods.
  • Strengthening traditional fisher institutions.
Explore Fisheries Work

Women Empowerment

Placing women at the center of community progress through leadership development, micro-enterprises, and institutional strengthening.

  • Strengthening Self-Help Groups (SHGs).
  • Vocational skill training & micro-enterprises.
  • Financial & digital literacy initiatives.
  • Promoting women leadership in local governance.
Explore Women Leadership

Coastal Conservation

Protecting critical marine habitats and safeguarding endangered coastal species through scientific monitoring and local stewardship.

  • Conserving the endangered Indian horseshoe crab (Tachypleus gigas).
  • Beach, estuary, & riverbank clean-up drives.
  • Plastic waste reduction & source segregation.
  • Citizen science & biodiversity mapping.
Explore Conservation Work

Disaster Preparedness

Empowering vulnerable coastal villages exposed to cyclones and floods to lead in local emergency preparedness and recovery.

  • Village Disaster Management Committees.
  • Search, rescue, & emergency response training.
  • Early warning dissemination systems.
  • Post-disaster livelihood rehabilitation.
Explore Resilience Work

WASH Initiatives

Tackling coastal salinity intrusion and water contamination by developing climate-resilient drinking water and sanitation solutions.

  • Safe drinking water access in saline zones.
  • Community sanitation infrastructure.
  • Hygiene behaviour change communication.
  • School menstrual hygiene management.
Explore WASH Initiatives

Community Health

Bringing preventive healthcare, early disease screening, and nutrition education closer to underserved rural and coastal populations.

  • Community health screening camps.
  • Maternal, newborn, & child health education.
  • Targeted anaemia prevention & nutrition.
  • Referral networks & chronic disease awareness.
Explore Health Services

Geographic Footprint

Where We Work

SRMSS operates across several rural and coastal districts of Odisha, addressing unique environmental and social vulnerabilities with a strong community presence.

  • Coastal Fishing Villages: Promoting participatory fisheries management and seasonal livelihood diversification.
  • Intertidal & Estuarine Habitats: Safeguarding critical spawning beaches for the endangered Indian horseshoe crab.
  • Salinity-Prone Communities: Developing climate-resilient drinking water and sanitation infrastructure.

Collaborative Network

Our Multi-Stakeholder Partnerships

We believe that lasting impact is achieved through collaboration. SRMSS works closely with women's groups, Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs), government health and fisheries departments, academic universities, and scientific researchers.

By bridging the gap between grassroots community knowledge and institutional expertise, we ensure our initiatives are scientifically sound, policy-aligned, and deeply supported by the people they serve.
