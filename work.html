Thematic Pillars

Our Work & Interventions

Practical, community-driven initiatives driving resilience and biodiversity conservation.

Pillar 01

Sustainable Fisheries

Sustainable fisheries are essential for protecting marine ecosystems while securing the livelihoods of coastal communities. SRMSS works with traditional fishing communities to promote responsible fishing practices, strengthen community institutions, and support the sustainable management of coastal and marine resources.

We build the capacity of fisher groups, improve access to government services, and support alternative income options during seasonal fishing ban periods to improve household resilience.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Community-based fisheries management & responsible fishing practices.
  • Capacity building of fisher groups and community institutions.
  • Alternative livelihood opportunities during fishing ban periods.
  • Improved adoption of sustainable fishing practices among coastal communities.
  • Diversified income opportunities for fishing families during lean seasons.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Strengthening Self-help Groups (SHGs) and leadership development.
  • Livelihood skill training and women-led micro-enterprise promotion.
  • Financial and digital literacy with market linkages for producers.
  • Awareness on gender equality, legal rights, health, and child protection.
  • Increased access to government welfare schemes and financial services.
Pillar 02

Women Empowerment

Women are central to the social and economic progress of every community. SRMSS works with women from rural and vulnerable communities to strengthen their leadership, improve livelihood opportunities, and increase access to resources, institutions, and government services.

We support women to build strong Self-Help Groups (SHGs), develop vocational skills, start small enterprises, improve financial literacy, and participate confidently in local governance.

Pillar 03

Coastal Ecosystem Conservation

Healthy coastal ecosystems support biodiversity, sustain livelihoods, and protect communities. We collaborate with coastal communities, academic institutions, and conservation partners to restore habitats through scientific evidence and local stewardship.

Horseshoe Crab Conservation

The Odisha coast is a vital habitat for the Indian horseshoe crab (Tachypleus gigas), a "living fossil" surviving over 450 million years. This IUCN Red List Endangered species is threatened by coastal development and accidental fishing capture.

  • Habitat monitoring and documentation of breeding sites.
  • Engagement with fishers to promote safe release.
  • Rescue and rehabilitation of stranded crabs.
  • Citizen science and biodiversity monitoring.

Coastal Waste Management

Plastic pollution and unmanaged solid waste pose serious threats to marine wildlife and public health. SRMSS promotes community-led waste management solutions that reduce waste entering rivers, estuaries, and the sea.

  • Beach, riverbank, and estuary clean-up drives.
  • Plastic waste reduction and source segregation.
  • Environmental education for schools and youth.
  • Promotion of recycling and circular economy practices.
Pillar 04

Disaster Preparedness

Coastal communities are increasingly exposed to cyclones, floods, and storm surges. SRMSS works with vulnerable communities to strengthen local preparedness, reduce disaster risks, and support timely response and recovery during emergencies.

By empowering communities to take the lead in preparedness planning and volunteer training, we help improve resilience and reduce the long-term impacts of disasters.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Formation of Village Disaster Management Committees.
  • Capacity building of community volunteers for emergency response.
  • Early warning dissemination and evacuation preparedness.
  • First aid, search and rescue, and emergency response training.
  • Relief coordination and humanitarian assistance during emergencies.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Improving access to safe drinking water in coastal communities.
  • Community sanitation infrastructure and sanitation awareness.
  • Hygiene promotion and behaviour change communication.
  • Menstrual hygiene awareness and school management programs.
  • Climate-resilient WASH solutions for salinity-prone coastal areas.
Pillar 05

WASH Initiatives

Access to safe drinking water, improved sanitation, and good hygiene practices is essential for coastal communities, where salinity intrusion, water contamination, and flooding often affect public health.

SRMSS improves WASH services through community participation, behaviour change communication, and collaboration with local governments. We also work with schools to create healthy learning environments.

Pillar 06

Community Health

Many rural and coastal communities face challenges in accessing preventive healthcare due to limited infrastructure and distance from medical facilities. SRMSS improves access by bringing health awareness, early screening, and community-based healthcare closer to underserved populations.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Community health camps and specialized outreach services.
  • Preventive health screening and early disease detection.
  • Maternal, newborn, and child health awareness programs.
  • Nutrition education and targeted anaemia prevention.
  • Awareness on non-communicable diseases (NCDs) and referral networks.
