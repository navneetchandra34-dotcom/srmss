Thematic Pillars

Our Work & Interventions

Practical, community-driven initiatives driving resilience and biodiversity conservation.

Pillar 01

Sustainable Fisheries

Sustainable fisheries are essential for protecting marine ecosystems while securing the livelihoods of coastal communities. SRMSS works with traditional fishing communities to promote responsible fishing practices, strengthen community institutions, and support the sustainable management of coastal and marine resources.

Our programmes encourage community participation in fisheries management, build the capacity of fisher groups, and improve access to government services and alternative income options during seasonal fishing ban periods, reducing pressure on marine resources while improving household resilience.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Community-based fisheries management & responsible fishing practices.
  • Capacity building of fisher groups and community institutions.
  • Alternative livelihood opportunities during fishing ban periods.
  • Access to government schemes and social protection.
  • Improved adoption of sustainable fishing practices among coastal communities.
  • Diversified income opportunities for fishing families during lean seasons.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Strengthening Self-help Groups (SHGs) and leadership development.
  • Livelihood skill training and women-led micro-enterprise promotion.
  • Financial and digital literacy with market linkages for producers.
  • Awareness on gender equality, legal rights, health, and child protection.
  • Increased access to government welfare schemes and financial services.
  • Greater participation of women in local decision-making and governance.
Pillar 02

Women Empowerment

Women are central to the social and economic progress of every community. SRMSS works with women from rural and vulnerable communities to strengthen their leadership, improve livelihood opportunities, and increase access to resources, institutions, and government services.

Our approach combines economic empowerment with social awareness. We support women to build strong Self-Help Groups (SHGs), develop vocational skills, start small enterprises, improve financial literacy, and participate confidently in household decision-making and local governance.

Pillar 03

Coastal Ecosystem Conservation

Healthy coastal ecosystems support biodiversity, sustain livelihoods, and protect communities from climate change impacts. We collaborate with coastal communities, academic institutions, and conservation partners to restore and protect habitats through scientific evidence and local stewardship.

Horseshoe Crab Conservation

The Odisha coast is a vital habitat for the Indian horseshoe crab (Tachypleus gigas), a "living fossil" surviving over 450 million years. As an indicator of intertidal habitat health, this IUCN Red List Endangered species is threatened by coastal development and accidental fishing capture.

  • Habitat monitoring, mapping, and documentation of breeding sites.
  • Engagement with fishers to reduce accidental capture and promote safe release.
  • Rescue and rehabilitation of stranded horseshoe crabs.
  • Citizen science and community-based biodiversity monitoring.

Coastal Waste Management

Plastic pollution and unmanaged solid waste pose serious threats to coastal ecosystems, marine wildlife, fisheries, and public health. SRMSS promotes community-led waste management solutions that reduce waste entering rivers, estuaries, and the sea.

  • Beach, riverbank, and estuary clean-up drives.
  • Plastic waste reduction and source segregation.
  • Environmental education for schools and youth.
  • Promotion of recycling and circular economy practices.
Pillar 04

Disaster Preparedness & Response

Coastal communities are increasingly exposed to cyclones, floods, storm surges, and climate-related disasters that threaten lives and essential infrastructure. SRMSS works with vulnerable communities to strengthen local preparedness, reduce disaster risks, and support timely response and recovery during emergencies.

By empowering communities to take the lead in preparedness planning and volunteer training, we help improve resilience and reduce the long-term impacts of disasters.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Formation and strengthening of Village Disaster Management Committees.
  • Capacity building of community volunteers for emergency response.
  • Early warning dissemination and evacuation preparedness.
  • First aid, search and rescue, and emergency response training.
  • Relief coordination and humanitarian assistance during emergencies.
  • Livelihood recovery and post-disaster rehabilitation support.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Improving access to safe drinking water in coastal communities.
  • Community sanitation infrastructure and sanitation awareness.
  • Hygiene promotion and behaviour change communication.
  • Menstrual hygiene awareness and school management programs.
  • Water conservation and sustainable water resource management.
  • Climate-resilient WASH solutions for salinity-prone coastal areas.
Pillar 05

WASH Initiatives

Access to safe drinking water, improved sanitation, and good hygiene practices is essential for coastal communities, where salinity intrusion, water contamination, flooding, and extreme weather often affect water security and public health.

SRMSS improves WASH services through community participation, behaviour change communication, and collaboration with local governments. We also work with schools to create healthy learning environments and promote menstrual hygiene management.

Pillar 06

Community Health

Good health is the foundation of resilient communities. Many rural and coastal communities face challenges in accessing preventive and primary healthcare due to limited health infrastructure, distance from medical facilities, and low awareness.

SRMSS improves access to essential health services by bringing health awareness, early screening, and community-based healthcare closer to underserved populations through partnerships with government departments and community volunteers.

Key Interventions & Measured Impact

  • Community health camps and specialized outreach services.
  • Preventive health screening and early disease detection.
  • Maternal, newborn, and child health awareness programs.
  • Nutrition education and targeted anaemia prevention.
  • Adolescent health and comprehensive life skills education.
  • Awareness on non-communicable diseases (NCDs) and referral networks.
