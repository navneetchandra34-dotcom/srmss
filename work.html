Pillar 01

Sustainable Fisheries

[span_58](start_span)

Sustainable fisheries are essential for protecting marine ecosystems while securing the livelihoods of coastal communities[span_58](end_span). [span_59](start_span)SRMSS works with traditional fishing communities to promote responsible fishing practices, strengthen community institutions, and support the sustainable management of coastal and marine resources[span_59](end_span).

[span_60](start_span)

Our programmes encourage community participation in fisheries management, build the capacity of fisher groups, and improve access to government services and livelihood opportunities[span_60](end_span). [span_61](start_span)We also support alternative income options during seasonal fishing ban periods, helping reduce pressure on marine resources[span_61](end_span).

Pillar 02

Women Empowerment

[span_75](start_span)

Women are central to the social and economic progress of every community[span_75](end_span). [span_76](start_span)SRMSS works with women from rural and vulnerable communities to strengthen their leadership, improve livelihood opportunities, and increase access to resources, institutions, and government services[span_76](end_span).

[span_77](start_span)

Our approach combines economic empowerment with social awareness[span_77](end_span). Pillar 03

Coastal Ecosystem Conservation

[span_79](start_span)

Healthy coastal ecosystems support biodiversity, sustain livelihoods, and protect communities from the impacts of climate change[span_79](end_span). [span_80](start_span)SRMSS works with coastal communities, government agencies, academic institutions, and conservation partners to restore and protect habitats through community participation and scientific evidence[span_80](end_span).

Horseshoe Crab Conservation

[span_81](start_span)

The Odisha coast is one of the most important habitats for the Indian horseshoe crab (*Tachypleus gigas*), often referred to as a "living fossil" because it has survived for more than 450 million years[span_81](end_span). [span_82](start_span)The species is included in the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species as **Endangered** due to habitat degradation, coastal development, and accidental fishing capture[span_82](end_span).

    [span_83](start_span)
  • Habitat monitoring, mapping, and documentation[span_83](end_span).
    • [span_84](start_span)[span_85](start_span)
  • Engagement with fishers to reduce accidental capture and promote safe release[span_84](end_span)[span_85](end_span).
    • [span_86](start_span)
  • Rescue and rehabilitation of stranded horseshoe crabs[span_86](end_span).
    • [span_87](start_span)
  • Citizen science and community-based biodiversity monitoring[span_87](end_span).
    • [span_88](start_span)
  • Protection of critical spawning and breeding habitats[span_88](end_span).

Pillar 05

Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH)

[span_114](start_span)

Access to safe drinking water, improved sanitation, and good hygiene practices is essential for the health and well-being of coastal communities, where salinity intrusion, water contamination, flooding, and extreme weather events often affect water security and public health[span_114](end_span).

[span_115](start_span)

SRMSS works with vulnerable coastal communities to improve WASH services through community participation, behaviour change, and collaboration with local governments and community institutions[span_115](end_span).

Community Health
